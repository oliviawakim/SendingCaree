Sending Care to Others

For moments that matter: Give the gift of Chick-fil-A.
Chick-fil-A’s Send Care to Others feature is a new ordering option that allows guests to customize a meal for someone special. Complete with exclusive packaging, add-ons, and a personalized note, care packages can be ordered through the app and delivered to the recipient’s doorstep with Care.

Logos

There are multiple iterations of the logo which can be used in various scenarios, but the full-color logo should be used most frequently. Iconography

When first deciding on iconography that would best illustrate these Sending Care features, we knew we needed to create a unique visual identity: one distinctive enough to convey the novelty of this Sending Care experience, yet still immediately recognizable and cohesively integrated into the Chick-fil-A mobile app design that guests already know and love. Our product icon depicts a set of giving hands – inspired by symbols already adopted into the client’s mobile app branding – with our new addition of a heart that represents Chick-fil-A’s vision of “care.”

By crafting several other complimentary icons throughout this process to help solidify this visual identity, our designs were created with intentional references to existing Chick-fil-A icons and thus blend in seamlessly when partnered with our other visual components used to flesh out the design of the new mobile app prototype.

Packaging design

We wanted the packaging to seem customizable for each special occasion. When the customer is ordering a care package, they can select the box design they want to use and the add-ons will go in the box. Since the delivery drivers need to keep the food warm, we created corresponding stickers to seal the bags shut so they match the care packages. When a customer orders a meal and a care package for a loved one, the delivery will include a bag with the sticker and a matching box with add-ons inside.
Finally, the inside of the box will be printed with the campaign’s slogan “for the moments that matter.”
